Research: CoDeS Lab

In my final year of my undergraduate degree I was a research assistant within the Cognitive Development Science (CoDeS) Lab at Virginia Tech. This lab is dedicated to researching how young children can learn effectively from various technologies and interfaces. Within the CoDeS Lab, I was able to explore new technologies like Eye-Tracking and Conversational AI, and conduct user research in an academic setting to gage our participants' cognitive and academic growth with such interfaces. In April 2023, my team and I were able to present our poster “Are you still watching on your phone or TV? The impact of mobile media on visual attention and learning,” at the Dennis Dean Undergraduate Research and Creative Scholarship Conference. Our abstract can be found on Page 215.

Research: Of Race and Caste Paper

With my love of Shakespeare, and my Indian culture, I was able to write a research paper as a passion project with the help of my faculty advisor, Dr. Katharine Cleland. This paper is entitled "Of Race and Caste: Discrimination Across Cultures in Shakespeare's Othello and Bhardwaj's Omkara". Within my paper, I explore the implications of Othello as a man of color in Shakespeare's work, and the discrimination he faces throughout the play. I utilize this analysis of Othello as a character to further examine how the Bollywood movie, Omkara, adapts Shakespeare's famous work into the setting of contemporary India, and converts the critique of racism into a critique of casteism. This paper was published in Philologia, and I was fortunate enough to be able to present my work at the ACC Meeting of the Minds, GPSS Research Symposium, and English Undergaduate Excellence Conference.
